Question 4What makes an industry "worth" a lot — how much we buy from it, or how much we need it?
✦

Neither, exactly.

An industry's value now is defined as value added: what a layer sells, minus what it buys from the other layers.

What makes a layer thick? Money flowing in from everyone who wants what it makes — families shopping, businesses buying tools, other countries buying exports. Family shopping is only about two-thirds of the cake.

Here is how an average family's $101,805 paycheck spent (2023).

The tax piece here is income taxes (BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey, 2023 — the newest year BLS published a tax number). Families also pay Social Security, sales and other taxes that hide inside the other pieces.

And "need" is a different question from a different measuring tool. A price only tells you what someone will pay for one more of something — which depends on how scarce it is, not on how much we'd miss it if it vanished. So a layer's dollar size measures how much money moves through it, not how important it is. It's a tape measure, not a report card.
