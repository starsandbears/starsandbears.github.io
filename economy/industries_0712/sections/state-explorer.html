Pick your stateIs it the same everywhere? Does my state make and do the same stuff?
✦

Nope — every state has its own flavor. Pick one and watch the charts change!

The country's cake is really 50 little cakes added up, and each state's looks different — lots of farming in one, oil or tech or tourism in another. Choose a state below: first you'll see its industry mix side-by-side with the whole country — what it leans on more or less than average — then four charts show its value added, how its layers grew over 20 years, and its jobs and pay by industry. (M2 and gross output are national totals, so those two don't split by state.)

🇺🇸 United States: each industry's share of the economy (value added, 2025), next to the US average (grey bars). Longer colored bars show what your state leans on more than the country.
💰 Value each industry adds to the United States's cake (GDP, 2025).
🕰️ How the United States's layers grew, 2005–2025 (value added per year, in each year's own dollars — so part of every climb is inflation). The thickest layers start visible; click a name in the legend to show or hide any layer.
👷 How many people work in each industry in the United States (thousands of jobs, 2025).
💵 Average pay per worker in the United States (2025), next to the US average (grey bars); dashed line = that state's poverty line for a family of 4 (2025 guideline: $32,150 — higher in Alaska & Hawaii).

Same real data as the national charts, split by state — GDP from BEA (2025); jobs & pay from BLS QCEW (2025; private industries are private-sector, "Government" is federal + state + local). A few tiny industries in small states aren't shown on the jobs & pay charts because the government withholds very small counts to protect privacy. Press 📷 Save PNG on any chart to download it as a picture.
