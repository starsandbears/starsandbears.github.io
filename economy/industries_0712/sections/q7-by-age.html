Question 7Will I spend money differently when I grow up?
✦

Yes — spending grows, peaks in your 40s–50s, then shrinks.

Spending climbs like a hill: small when you're young, biggest around age 45–54 (~$100,000 a year!), then lower in retirement. And the mix shifts — the young spend more on getting around, families on housing and food, and older people more and more on health care (its share grows from about 3% under 25 to over 14% at 75+).

And here's the part that sneaks up on you: as you grow up and (hopefully!) get richer, a smaller share of your money goes to the must-haves — a 170-year-old rule called Engel's law. Food, a home and health care occupy about 60% to 40% of household spending. And spending grows slower than income — although the top fifth earns more than 10×, the spends is only about 4× as much.
