🧒
Question 7Will I spend money differently when I grow up?
✦

Yes — spending grows, peaks in your 40s–50s, then shrinks.

Spending climbs like a hill: small when you're young, biggest around age 45–54 (~$100,000 a year!), then lower in retirement. And the mix shifts — the young spend more on getting around, families on housing and food, and older people more and more on health care (its share grows from about 3% under 25 to over 14% at 75+).

And here's the part that sneaks up on you: as you grow up and (hopefully!) get richer, a smaller share of your money goes to the must-haves — a 170-year-old rule called Engel's law. Must-haves = food + shelter + utilities + health care - eating 60¢ to 40¢ of every $1 the family spends. And spending grows slower than income: the top fifth earns about 2.5× the average family's income but spends only about 1.9× as much — while the bottom fifth earns just a sixth of the average yet still spends nearly half as much.

Numbers: BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey, 2024 — Table 1101 (quintiles of income before taxes);
