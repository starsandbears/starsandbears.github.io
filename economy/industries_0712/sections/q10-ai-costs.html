Question 10Everyone keeps talking about AI — could that make everything cheaper?
✦

Some things, yes — AI is a super-fast helper that makes more with less. But it can't make more land.

Think of AI as a tireless helper that can do certain jobs much faster — writing, coding, answering questions, spotting patterns. When you can make more of something with less effort, that's exactly the "more supply" that pulls prices down (remember Question 9). It's the same reason clothes and gadgets got cheap — machines made mountains of them. So for information-y things — apps, help desks, translations, a lot of paperwork — AI could make them cheaper and better.

And here's a catch grown-ups argue about: when AI makes a company's work cheaper, do the savings turn into lower prices (if lots of companies compete) or bigger profits (if only a few own the best AI)? New tools also take years to spread. So AI is a powerful supply booster — it'll make plenty of things cheaper and better — but it's a helper, not magic, and it won't make the truly scarce things cheap on its own.
