Question 8Does our family spend the same as families in other states?
✦

No — where you live changes both how much you spend and on what.

People in richer, pricier states spend more per person; people in cheaper states spend less. On this map, darker means more spending per person each year — the Northeast and West Coast glow darkest, the South is lighter.

Spending per person by state, 2024 (BEA). Darker = more. Hover a state. (Alaska & Hawaii left off to keep the map tidy.)

And the mix shifts too. Pick any state below to see how its spending splits across the big categories — housing takes a giant bite in expensive states, and the slices look different where homes are cheaper.

Per-person spending by category (BEA PCE by state, 2024), next to the US average (grey) when a state is picked. What's still in "Everything else": personal care, tobacco, magazines, jewelry, donations to churches & charities, and other odds and ends.

Heads up — a different ruler. This state chart uses BEA "PCE": spending per person, and it counts money spent for you too (like the part of a doctor bill your insurance or the government pays). So its numbers run bigger — especially health care — than the family-budget pizzas above, which come from a survey of what households pay out of their own pockets. It's the only spending data that exists for every state, so it's great for comparing places — just not an apples-to-apples match with the family budget.
