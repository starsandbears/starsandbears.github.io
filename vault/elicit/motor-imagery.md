# Motor Imagery

Motor imagery (MI) refers to the mental simulation of a movement without any
actual motor output. Imagined movements modulate sensorimotor rhythms in
[[eeg]], producing event-related desynchronization (ERD) over contralateral
motor cortex that BCI systems can detect and classify.

## Overview

Typical MI paradigms ask the user to imagine left-hand, right-hand, foot, or
tongue movements. Classifiers such as Common Spatial Patterns (CSP) followed
by LDA or Riemannian methods are standard.
