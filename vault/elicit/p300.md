# P300

The P300 is a positive deflection in the [[eeg]] occurring roughly 300 ms after
a rare, task-relevant stimulus. It is one of the most studied [[erp]]
components and underlies a major family of BCI spellers.

## Overview

The P300 is most reliably elicited by the [[oddball-paradigm]], in which a
participant attends to infrequent target stimuli embedded in a stream of
frequent standards. Its amplitude scales with the rarity and salience of the
target, and its latency reflects stimulus evaluation time.

## In BCI

The [[bci-speller]] popularized by Farwell and Donchin (1988) uses a
row/column flashing matrix; the row and column eliciting the largest P300
identify the user's intended character.
