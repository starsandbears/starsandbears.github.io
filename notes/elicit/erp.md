# Event-Related Potential (ERP)

An ERP is a measured brain response that is the direct result of a specific
sensory, cognitive, or motor event. ERPs are extracted from ongoing [[eeg]]
by time-locking and averaging trials to the event onset.

## Overview

Because individual ERP components are small relative to background EEG, they
are typically revealed by averaging over many trials. Well-known components
include the [[p300]], N170, and N400, each associated with different cognitive
processes.
