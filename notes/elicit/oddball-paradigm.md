# Oddball Paradigm

The oddball paradigm is an experimental design in which infrequent "target"
stimuli are interspersed within a sequence of frequent "standard" stimuli.
The participant's task is typically to detect or count the targets.

## Overview

This paradigm reliably elicits the [[p300]] ERP component, with the amplitude
of the response inversely related to the probability of the target. It is the
workhorse of attention and ERP research and the basis for several BCI
control schemes.
